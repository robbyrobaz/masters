The 4 Rounds — Same Data, Different Results

We discovered that how you frame data matters as much as the data itself. The same player stats produced completely different AI consensus depending on editorial framing in the prompt.

Key Finding: Round 2 used phrases like "Scheffler CRASHED," "Reed = contrarian gold," and "AVOID" labels. This swung Tier 1 from 67% Scheffler to 90% Fitzpatrick. When the same facts were presented neutrally in Round 4, Scheffler returned to #1 at 41%. AI models are highly susceptible to editorial framing.

What We Learned

1. AI consensus is only as unbiased as the prompt
2. "30 models agree" can be an echo chamber if you lead the question
3. The picks that survive all framings are the real signal (Conners, Berger)
4. Models with their own knowledge found things we missed (Rahm's surge, Lee's form)
5. Always run multiple rounds with different framings before trusting a consensus
